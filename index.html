Introduction to the Big Bang

The Big Bang is the scientific theory that explains the origins of the universe. It is based on the idea that the universe began as a hot and dense point, which rapidly expanded and cooled over time. The Big Bang is an important theory because it helps us understand the history and evolution of the universe.

History of the Universe

The universe is believed to be around 13.8 billion years old. It began with the Big Bang, which occurred around 13.8 billion years ago. After the Big Bang, the universe rapidly expanded and cooled, leading to the formation of galaxies, stars, and planets. About 4.6 billion years ago, our own solar system formed, and life on Earth began around 3.5 billion years ago.
